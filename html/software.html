Aaron Hazzard's Software Engineering Path


Why Software Engineering?


As a young boy growing up in front of the television a lot. Aaron has seen a lot of films that consist of persons hacking into computer systems and this is what drew his attention to code. As he saw these awesome films with hackers pulling up their laptops typing random green letters on the screen that made absolutely no sense to him. He asked himself,

“I wonder how all of this works, what do those text on the screen really do, is this intelligence something that you naturally need to be born with?”

Ever since he had that thought, he decided that he would really love to learn how to hack computer systems, but first, he must have learned what is and THE BASICS OF CODING. Later in his life he developed the logic for coding and realized that it is nothing like the what the movies portray. He realized that coding, in reality, is a lot more complicated than it looks in the movies and its not as easy as pulling up your laptop and cracking a code within a few seconds.

I've mentioned hacking, but what about software engineering? Coding/Hacking compliments software engineering and with the skills he developed he was already ready to become a software engineer. However, there weren't many free learning sources for him to completely learn ethical hacking/Cyber Security so he gave up on that dream and decided to stick with what he loves today - Software Engineering.
